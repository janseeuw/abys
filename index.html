About Me

Image credits: Misha Vallejo Prut

I’m Ana Belén Yánez Suárez — a marine biologist, National Geographic Explorer, and PhD candidate at Memorial University of Newfoundland, and a former IMBRSea scholar. I study coral reef ecosystems across depths, from shallow tropical reefs in the Pacific and Caribbean to deep-sea coral communities thriving in low-oxygen waters.

My fascination with corals deepened in the Galápagos, where I contributed to the first systematic characterization of deep-sea benthic fauna on unexplored seamounts. I later led coral restoration efforts and studied coral spawning in the Caribbean, followed by research on the combined effects of warming and eutrophication on Red Sea corals.

My Research

Concerned by the rapid deoxygenation of deep tropical waters especially around the Galápagos and the Easter Tropical Pacific in general I am focusing on cold-water coral ecosystems. I lead the project Cold-Water Coral Distribution Across Oxygen Minimum Zones in the Galápagos and Costa Rica, supported by a National Geographic Society grant. As part of this work, I led the discovery of a monospecific yellow coral garden thriving in anoxic conditions near Isla del Coco during a Falkor (too) expedition Schmidt Ocean Institute Expedition. This effort was carried out in collaboration with scientists from the University of Costa Rica and the Charles Darwin Research Station.

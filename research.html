Research Projects

My research focuses on understanding marine ecosystems and developing innovative conservation strategies. Through fieldwork, laboratory analysis, and collaborative partnerships, I explore the complexities of ocean life and work to protect endangered marine species.

Research Areas

Deep-Sea Biodiversity Mapping

This ongoing project aims to document and map deep-sea biodiversity in understudied regions of the Pacific Ocean. Using advanced submersible technology and environmental DNA analysis, we're creating comprehensive biodiversity inventories that inform conservation planning.

Project Details

The Deep-Sea Biodiversity Mapping project began in 2019 as a collaboration between our research team at Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution and international partners from Japan, Chile, and New Zealand. The project focuses on mapping biodiversity in deep-sea environments below 1000 meters, with particular emphasis on seamounts, hydrothermal vents, and abyssal plains.

Using a combination of remotely operated vehicles (ROVs), environmental DNA sampling, and traditional collection methods, we've documented over 200 species, including 15 that are new to science. Our findings have significant implications for understanding deep-sea ecosystem functioning and for designing effective marine protected areas in international waters.

Key Findings

  • Discovery of previously unknown hydrothermal vent communities in the South Pacific
  • Documentation of unique adaptations in deep-sea organisms to extreme pressure and temperature conditions
  • Identification of biodiversity hotspots that warrant protection from deep-sea mining activities
  • Development of new methodologies for non-invasive biodiversity assessment using environmental DNA

Coral Reef Resilience

This project investigates factors that contribute to coral reef resilience in the face of climate change. By studying reefs that have survived bleaching events, we're identifying genetic, ecological, and environmental factors that enhance resilience.

Project Details

The Coral Reef Resilience project was initiated in 2020 in response to increasing coral bleaching events worldwide. Our research focuses on identifying coral populations that demonstrate unusual resilience to thermal stress and understanding the mechanisms behind this resilience.

Field sites include reefs in the Galápagos Islands, the Great Barrier Reef, and the Caribbean, allowing for comparative analysis across different ocean basins. We combine field observations with laboratory experiments and genomic analysis to develop a comprehensive understanding of resilience factors.

Key Findings

  • Identification of specific coral genotypes with enhanced thermal tolerance
  • Documentation of symbiotic relationships with certain algal species that improve coral survival during heat stress
  • Evidence that local management actions, such as reducing pollution and fishing pressure, can significantly enhance reef resilience
  • Development of predictive models to identify reefs with high resilience potential for prioritized protection

Marine Mammal Conservation

This project focuses on tracking and studying endangered marine mammal populations to inform conservation efforts. Using non-invasive monitoring techniques, we're documenting migration patterns, habitat use, and population dynamics.

Project Details

The Marine Mammal Conservation project began in 2021 with a focus on endangered whale and dolphin species in the Eastern Pacific. Our research employs a combination of satellite tracking, acoustic monitoring, and photo identification to study these charismatic marine mammals without disturbing their natural behaviors.

A key innovation in this project is the development and deployment of minimally invasive tracking devices that can be attached to animals briefly during natural surfacing events. These devices provide unprecedented data on diving behavior, feeding patterns, and migration routes.

Key Findings

  • Documentation of previously unknown migration corridors for blue whales between feeding and breeding grounds
  • Identification of critical habitat areas that overlap with shipping lanes, informing marine spatial planning
  • Evidence of changing distribution patterns in response to shifting prey availability due to climate change
  • Development of improved population estimates for several endangered cetacean species

Research Methods

Field Sampling

Collection of biological specimens and environmental data using specialized equipment for deep-sea and shallow-water environments.

Genomic Analysis

DNA sequencing and bioinformatic analysis to study biodiversity, evolutionary relationships, and adaptive mechanisms.

Remote Sensing

Use of satellite data and underwater sensors to monitor environmental conditions and track animal movements.

ROV Exploration

Deployment of remotely operated vehicles to explore and document deep-sea environments that are inaccessible to human divers.

Data Modeling

Statistical analysis and predictive modeling to understand patterns and trends in marine ecosystems.

Community Science

Engagement with local communities and citizen scientists to collect data and implement conservation initiatives.
